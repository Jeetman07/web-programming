About Mount Everest

Mount Everest, the highest mountain on Earth, stands proudly in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Tibet (China). Rising to an incredible height of 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level. It is a symbol of natural grandeur and human aspiration. Known as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, Everest holds deep spiritual and cultural significance for the local people. Its breathtaking beauty lies in its towering snow-covered peak, gleaming glaciers, and ever-changing skies that shift from golden hues at sunrise to deep blues by night. For climbers, Mount Everest represents the ultimate challenge — a place where courage, endurance, and determination are tested against the raw power of nature. Beyond its physical height, Everest stands as a timeless reminder of humanity’s desire to reach the greatest heights, both literally and spiritually.
